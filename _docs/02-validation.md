Caveats 

As we want to focus on developing the app with the users first in mind, we mainly focused on discovering our clients. We distributed a form, and as our colleagues are the easiest target for first adopters, we want them to describe what their current experience is regarding event searching and attending.
The other side of our clients, the event organizers, are having a difficult time as the HORECA industry is struggling. We are having a hard time connecting with them, and as the pandemic situation might last for a few months, we are waiting for a little in order for them to stabilize and have insightful communication. 

Another step that takes longer than expected is getting in touch with artists and influencers. We want to target small and growing performers, and also fully established artists. We are still working on it :)



Questions :

As we wanted to make the form completion as pleasant as it can be, we chose a less formal approach and adapted the questions to social trends, and give them more flexibility with open questions. 

We can split the theme of the questions into three categories :

Where do you find the events?
How do you interact with said events?
How do you manage the social aspect of going out? 


Given these insights, we pushed the users to express their genuine experiences, and we searched for the pain points that appear the most.


Results :

The responders were between 20 to 23 years old, with 37.5 % percent of them being women.

The first question, and one of the most important ones, is about the source where they find events.
<img src="assets/img/validation/i1.png">

As we can see, the majority of them are using social media channels, such as Facebook, Youtube ads, and other platforms. The second most voted option is word of mouth, and the rest are traditional advertising methods that are losing momentum.



The follow-up questions are about satisfaction and discovery rate. A simple look confirms that more than half of people are barely satisfied with these methods, and frustration can build up, especially when the FOMO starts settling in, as people lose oportuinites. 


Following up, we wanted to know if the attendees go alone or gather a group of friends with them.


As we can see above, over 90% go only in groups of friends, and the main communication tool is direct messages inside chat groups.







Seeing this, we wanted to discover how efficient communication is. Needless to say, 75% of them are not happy with the current way of self-organization.



Continuing on this line of discovery, we want to know how they interact with other unknown attendees.

Here we can see that tend of socializing grows, as reassurance from the group of friends gives them a boost of socialization desire. 






Our last point of interest was to gain direct feedback on what can be improved in an event.
We selected a few relevant ones, as the other ones are related.



From this validation, we drew the following conclusions :

The current state of discovering events is lackluster and can be improved.
People don’t want to lose on events.
People go out more when they group up.
Grouping is done with messages and is really complicated and frustrating 
Events are missing direct feedback and other gimmicks that can please the attendees.


As we established in the first sections, Shibui is focused to resolve these pain points and create an alternative to the status quo of today.
